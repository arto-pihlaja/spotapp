## Technique Execution: SCAMPER Method

### Emerging Product Philosophy

Through the SCAMPER exploration, a clear product identity emerged: **map-first, community-created, signal-over-noise, anti-social-media, respect-the-user.** SpotApp is a coordination tool, not a social media platform. Maximum signal, zero noise.

### Key Design Principles Identified

- **Community-first data**: No editorial content, no external forecast dependencies at launch. Users create all content.
- **Map IS the app**: No tabs or separate sections. Everything is spatial.
- **Structured over unstructured**: Condition data through sliders/vectors, not free-text chat.
- **Value before registration**: Anonymous browse, register only to contribute.
- **Multi-sport by default**: Surfers, wing foilers, kitesurfers, SUP -- all first-class citizens.
- **Anti-social-media**: No feed, no likes, no streaks, no engagement guilt. A tool you pick up and put down.
- **Ephemeral by design**: Data has tiered lifespans. The app is always fresh.

### S -- Substitute

**[Substitute #1]**: Wiki Spots
_Concept_: Each surf spot has a collaboratively-edited wiki section -- tide specifics, hazards, local etiquette, parking tips, best wind directions, board recommendations. Community-maintained, version-controlled, always evolving.
_Novelty_: Replaces static editorial guides with living knowledge that improves over time. The more surfers use a spot, the richer its wiki becomes.

**[Substitute #2]**: Live Human Forecasts
_Concept_: Layer real-time user condition reports ("chest high, clean, light offshore, uncrowded") on top of algorithmic baseline forecasts. The forecast becomes a conversation, not a broadcast.
_Novelty_: Substitutes the authority of a forecast model with ground truth from people actually in the water.

**[Substitute #3]**: Social Session Planning
_Concept_: See who's planning to surf where and when. The "who's coming" layer turns spot selection from a solo weather-check into a social coordination moment.
_Novelty_: Substitutes the lonely "check forecast, drive to beach, hope for the best" pattern with a social signal.

**[Substitute #4]**: The Anti-Group-Chat
_Concept_: Consolidate the scattered WhatsApp/text coordination ("anyone heading to X?", "how's it looking at Y?") into a single location-aware, purpose-built interface. Session planning, condition reports, and social signals all in one place, organized by spot instead of buried in chat threads.
_Novelty_: Substitutes chaotic multi-channel communication with structured, map-based coordination. Information is persistent and location-tagged instead of disappearing up a chat scroll.

**[Substitute #5]**: Community-Scoped Trust
_Concept_: Start with a small local crew where everyone already knows each other. Trust is implicit. No need for reputation systems or verification -- the community IS the verification. Scale trust mechanisms only when the community outgrows personal familiarity.
_Novelty_: Substitutes the typical "build trust systems from day one" approach with real-world social trust. Grow features alongside community size.

### C -- Combine

**[Combine #6]**: Time-Slider Map
_Concept_: The map has a temporal dimension. Slide forward to see planned sessions ("3 people at Spot X at 7am tomorrow"), slide back to see condition history ("yesterday at 4pm: waist high, choppy"). Planning and history live on the same map, just at different times.
_Novelty_: Combines calendar/planning with spatial visualization. Nobody's putting a time scrubber on a surf map.

**[Combine #7]**: Snap-Report Fusion
_Concept_: Condition reports are photo-first with optional text. A quick camera snap from the parking lot IS the report. Photos have a limited lifespan on the feed (24h) so they're always fresh. The spot page always shows the most recent photo up top.
_Novelty_: Combines Instagram-stories ephemerality with practical condition reporting. The vanishing timeline keeps the feed relevant, not cluttered with last week's photos.

**[Combine #8]**: Social Media Bridging
_Concept_: Users can link external content (Instagram posts, YouTube clips) to a specific spot and time. Your sick barrel shot on Instagram gets pinned to the spot where it happened. The spot page becomes a curated gallery pulled from across platforms.
_Novelty_: Combines external social media with location context. SpotApp doesn't need to BE Instagram -- it just needs to be the spatial index for surf content that already exists elsewhere.

**[Combine #9]**: Vendor Event Pins
_Concept_: Local surf shops, shapers, and brands can drop event pins on spots -- "Board demo day Saturday 9am at Spot X." Events appear on the map timeline alongside session plans. No separate vendor system -- vendors are just users with commercial intent using the same session-planning mechanism.
_Novelty_: Combines community coordination with local commerce. Creates a natural path to partnerships and possibly revenue, while adding genuine value to users.

**[Combine #10]**: Unified Timeline Actors
_Concept_: Everyone -- surfers, shops, instructors, photographers -- uses the same session-planning mechanism. A session is just: who + where + when + optional comment. "Rob's Surfgear, Spot X, Sat 10-16, come try the new Duotone" and "Hemmu, Spot X, Thu 17-19" are the same object. No separate event system needed.
_Novelty_: Radically simple. Avoids building two systems (events vs sessions). The community naturally self-organizes commercial and social activity through one pattern.

**[Combine #11]**: Dual Photo Lifecycle
_Concept_: Photos have two tracks -- ephemeral (24h social feed, "what's it like right now?") and permanent (wiki-attached, "what does the parking look like?"). User chooses at upload time, or a wiki editor can "promote" a great social photo to permanent status.
_Novelty_: Combines the freshness pressure of stories-style content with the lasting value of a knowledge base. Same upload flow, different shelf life.

### A -- Adapt

**[Adapt #12]**: Crowd Magnetism / Crowd Avoidance Toggle
_Concept_: The "people going" indicator serves dual purposes depending on context. In tight-knit communities, it's a magnet ("3 friends heading out at 5pm, join them"). At scale, it's also a distribution tool. The same data, different user intent.
_Novelty_: Adapts Waze congestion from "avoid this" to "this is where the action is" -- the meaning flips based on community culture.

**[Adapt #13]**: Spot-Anchored Comments
_Concept_: Every user message is attached to a spot. No global feed, no random chat. When you open a spot, you see its timeline of updates, plans, and commentary. The spot IS the conversation.
_Novelty_: Adapts forum/channel structure but inverts it -- location is the organizing principle, not topic or group. Prevents the app from becoming "yet another chat."

**[Adapt #14]**: Human Sensor Network
_Concept_: Structured condition inputs -- swell height slider (0-2m), wind vector (direction + strength 0-20 m/s), maybe tide state, water temp, wave quality rating. Each user "votes" with their sliders, and the spot page shows an aggregated consensus with sample size. "Current conditions based on 4 reports in the last hour: 0.8m, NW 5m/s."
_Novelty_: Adapts the concept of crowdsourced sensor data (like Waze speed reports) into tactile, surf-specific input controls. Structured data enables aggregation, trending, and eventually pattern recognition.

**[Adapt #15]**: Wiki Version Control
_Concept_: Every wiki edit is attributed to a registered user, timestamped, and reversible. Adapts GitHub/Wikipedia's edit history model. In a small community, visible authorship IS moderation -- everyone sees who wrote what.
_Novelty_: Adapts developer tooling (version control) for community knowledge management. Lightweight enough for MVP but scales with community growth.

### M -- Modify / Magnify / Minimize

**[Modify #16]**: Map-First Architecture
_Concept_: The map IS the app. No tabs, no separate sections. Spots, people, conditions, sessions, photos, events -- all are layers on the map. Zoom level controls information density: zoomed out shows only major spots with activity counts, zoomed in reveals individual sessions, comments, condition reports.
_Novelty_: Most apps treat the map as one feature among many. This makes spatial context the single organizing principle for everything.

**[Modify #17]**: Zoom-Density Scaling
_Concept_: Information adapts to zoom level like a cartographic system. Continental zoom: only legendary spots. Regional zoom: popular spots with user counts. Local zoom: every break, individual session plans, live condition data, photos. The map teaches itself to users through natural zoom behavior.
_Novelty_: Magnifies map UX into an information architecture strategy. Solves the "too much data" problem elegantly without filters or settings.

**[Modify #18]**: Ghost Profile
_Concept_: Profile is name, optional photo, optional external link, and auto-generated activity summary. No bio, no wall, no friends list. "Hemmu -- 43 sessions since Jan 2025, frequents Spot X." Your surfing IS your identity.
_Novelty_: Minimizes social vanity features to zero. Anti-Facebook by design. The app knows you through what you do, not what you claim.

**[Modify #19]**: Ephemeral Data Architecture
_Concept_: Three tiers of data lifespan. Tier 1 -- live: condition reports, photos, comments (24-48h, then purged). Tier 2 -- medium: session records as simple tuples {user, spot, time} (months). Tier 3 -- permanent: aggregated stats ("203 sessions since 2024") and wiki content.
_Novelty_: Minimizes storage and clutter by design. The app is always fresh because old data self-destructs. Only the distilled essence persists.

**[Modify #20]**: Sunset Horizon Default
_Concept_: The default time view is "now until sunset" -- the only planning horizon that matters for surfing today. Tomorrow starts fresh at sunrise. The app breathes with the day cycle.
_Novelty_: Modifies the typical "show everything" approach into something that matches the natural rhythm of surfing. The app has a daily heartbeat.

### P -- Put to Other Uses

**[Put to Other Uses #21]**: Multi-Sport Water Platform
_Concept_: Surfers, wing foilers, kitesurfers, windsurfers, SUP paddlers -- all share the same spots but care about different condition parameters. A wing foiler wants strong wind, a surfer wants it light. Same map, same community features, but condition relevance adapts to sport context.
_Novelty_: One platform serves multiple tribes that already overlap in real life. No need for separate apps per sport -- they're already at the same beach.

**[Put to Other Uses #22]**: Sport-Aware Condition Display
_Concept_: The same wind report means different things to different users. 15 m/s onshore is terrible for surfing but perfect for wing foiling. Could the structured condition data show a simple "good for you" indicator based on user's sport preference.
_Novelty_: Same data, personalized relevance. Turns raw conditions into actionable signal per discipline.

**[Put to Other Uses #23]**: Environmental Data Integration
_Concept_: Rather than building monitoring, integrate existing environmental APIs -- water temperature, air quality, UV index, jellyfish warnings, algae alerts -- as additional spot data layers. Curate, don't create.
_Novelty_: The app becomes a hub that aggregates external data sources alongside community reports. Build the glue, not the sensors.

### E -- Eliminate

**[Eliminate #24]**: Anonymous Browse, Named Contribute
_Concept_: Anyone can open the app and explore -- spots, wiki, condition summaries, session counts. No signup wall. But usernames only appear when you register and contribute. Anonymous users see aggregated data ("4 people planning sessions") but not who.
_Novelty_: Eliminates the signup-before-value barrier. Discovery is free. Identity is earned through participation.

**[Eliminate #25]**: No Social Feed
_Concept_: No posts. No feed. No timeline to scroll. No "catch up" anxiety. The app shows: the map, spot data, aggregated conditions, planned sessions. That's it. Communication happens through structured actions (condition reports, session plans), not unstructured chat.
_Novelty_: Eliminates the single most addictive and time-wasting pattern in modern apps. SpotApp respects your time -- check in, get the picture, get out, go surf.

**[Eliminate #26]**: No Engagement Guilt
_Concept_: No streaks, no "you haven't posted in 3 days," no follower counts, no likes. The app is a tool you pick up when useful and put down without consequence. Activity stats exist for your own reference, not as social currency.
_Novelty_: Eliminates the dopamine manipulation layer. Anti-addictive by design. Ironic competitive advantage -- people trust and return to tools that don't try to trap them.

### R -- Reverse / Rearrange

**[Reverse #27]**: Opt-In Silence
_Concept_: Default notification state is zero. Nothing. Users opt into notifications per spot: "Tell me when someone plans a session at Spot X." You curate your own signal.
_Novelty_: Reverses the typical "notify everything, let user disable" pattern. Respect is the default.

**[Reverse #28]**: Community-Born Spots
_Concept_: No pre-loaded spot database. Users drop pins to create spots. A spot starts as just a pin and a name. Over time it accumulates wiki content, photos, condition history, sessions. Spots are born empty and grow organically.
_Novelty_: Reverses the editorial "we define the spots" model. The community's collective knowledge IS the database. Also solves the cold-start content problem -- you don't need to pre-populate anything.

**[Reverse #29]**: Value-First Funnel
_Concept_: Open app, see map, explore spots, read wikis, see activity. All in seconds, no registration. The moment you think "I want to post conditions" or "I want to mark my session" -- that's when registration appears. Motivation-triggered signup.
_Novelty_: Reverses the registration wall. Turns onboarding from a gate into a natural desire.

**[Reverse #30]**: No Forecast Dependency
_Concept_: Launch with zero external data integrations. The only condition data is from humans on the ground. This is both a philosophical choice (community-first) and a practical one (no API costs, no competing with Windy's UX). External forecasts can layer in later as supplementary, never primary.
_Novelty_: Reverses the assumption that a water sports app needs forecast data. The community IS the forecast.

## Technique Execution: Role Playing

Six personas were used to stress-test the SCAMPER ideas and uncover feature gaps. The personas largely confirmed the core design rather than revealing major holes -- a sign of architectural strength.

### Persona 1: Mika -- The Dawn Patrol Local

_Daily surfer, knows all spots, coordinates with a crew of 4-5 regulars via text. Opens app at 5:15 AM to decide whether to get up and where to go._

**[Role Playing #31]**: Recency Indicator
_Concept_: Each spot shows how fresh its latest condition report is -- "12 min ago," "3h ago," "no reports today." Prominently displayed, possibly color-coded: green (< 1h), yellow (1-4h), grey (stale).
_Novelty_: Makes data freshness a first-class UX element. Users instantly know which spots have live intel.

**[Role Playing #32]**: At-A-Glance Spot Card
_Concept_: Without tapping into a spot, the map shows a compact card: latest conditions summary (swell + wind), last report time, number of planned sessions in the next few hours, and optionally the most recent photo thumbnail. Everything needed for a go/no-go decision visible at map level.
_Novelty_: No drilling down required. The map surface itself carries actionable decision data.

**[Role Playing #33]**: Watching / Eyeing Status
_Concept_: A lightweight pre-commitment state: "I'm eyeing this spot for tomorrow dawn." Softer than a planned session, stronger than nothing. Others see the interest and it cascades. Lowers the coordination friction below even a WhatsApp message.
_Novelty_: Adds a third state between silence and commitment. Captures the real-world "I might go" that drives group coordination.

**[Role Playing #34]**: Social Signal as Dark-Hours Value
_Concept_: When conditions can't be visually confirmed (pre-dawn, fog), the app leans into what it CAN show: who's planning to go, who's watching, and last known conditions. The value proposition shifts from "conditions intel" to "coordination intel" depending on time of day.
_Novelty_: The app's value proposition is context-aware. It knows what's useful when.

### Persona 2: Sofia -- The Traveling Wing Foiler

_Just arrived in the area for two weeks. Has gear, doesn't know anyone, doesn't know the spots or local wind patterns. Downloads SpotApp at her Airbnb._

**[Role Playing #35]**: Color-Coded Activity Heat
_Concept_: Spots on the map glow or color-shift based on current activity level. A spot with 23 check-ins pulses with life. A quiet spot is muted. Strangers can read the map like a heat map without any local knowledge.
_Novelty_: Visual activity encoding lets strangers navigate an unfamiliar coast intuitively.

**[Role Playing #36]**: Stranger-Friendly Spot Page
_Concept_: The spot page serves two audiences simultaneously. Locals see the live social layer (who's coming, latest reports). Strangers see the wiki layer first -- parking, access, hazards, conditions guidance, skill level notes. Same page, both layers coexist.
_Novelty_: Naturally onboards strangers through persistent wiki content while serving regulars through live data. No separate "beginner mode" needed.

**[Role Playing #37]**: Planned Sessions as Implicit Recommendation
_Concept_: Sofia sees "4 people planning sessions at 10am" and that's enough to go. Other people's plans function as implicit endorsement -- no review system, no ratings, no algorithm needed.
_Novelty_: People who know this area are going here. That IS the recommendation engine.

**[Role Playing #38]**: Sport-Per-Session Tag
_Concept_: At check-in, a simple dropdown: surfing, wing foiling, kitesurfing, SUP, windsurf, other. Attached to the session, not the profile. The map shows "3 surfers, 1 wing foiler planning at Spot X."
_Novelty_: Acknowledges multi-sport reality. One user, many disciplines. The sport tag lives on the action, not the identity.

**[Role Playing #39]**: Objective Measures Over Subjective Ratings
_Concept_: Condition reports use estimated measurements (swell height in meters, wind speed in m/s, wind direction) rather than quality ratings. Everyone's guess of "0.7m swell" is roughly comparable. Everyone's idea of "good conditions" is not.
_Novelty_: Avoids rating subjectivity. Raw data lets each user apply their own sport-specific judgment.

**[Role Playing #40]**: Traveler Return Value
_Concept_: Sofia's past activity lives in her ghost profile. When she travels to another coast, the same app and patterns work. She becomes an ambassador who seeds new spots wherever she travels.
_Novelty_: The app's value compounds with geography. Every new coast visited, SpotApp is already useful if there's any local community.

### Persona 3: Janne -- The Nervous Beginner

_Bought first surfboard two months ago. Terrified of getting in the way or choosing a spot that's too advanced._

**[Role Playing #41]**: Spot Difficulty Context in Wiki
_Concept_: Wiki pages naturally include skill-level notes: "beginner-friendly sandy bottom," "advanced -- rocks and strong current." Not a formal rating system, just community-written context for self-selection.
_Novelty_: No patronizing "beginner mode." The wiki serves all levels through honest descriptions.

**[Role Playing #42]**: Peer Visibility for Beginners
_Concept_: Janne sees another beginner he knows is planning a session at the beginner-friendly spot. He marks his session too. The same social coordination mechanism works at all skill levels.
_Novelty_: The social layer is skill-level agnostic. No separate "beginner community" needed.

**Key insight:** Janne confirmed the app doesn't need special beginner features -- the existing design (wiki + session visibility) already serves him.

### Persona 4: Rob -- The Surf Shop Owner

_Runs a local shop. Wants to announce board demo days and sales._

**[Role Playing #43]**: Session Comment as Micro-Announcement
_Concept_: The optional comment field on a session plan is the only channel for context beyond who/where/when. "Rob's Surfgear, Spot X, Sat 10-16, come try the new Duotone." One line, tied to physical presence, naturally constrained.
_Novelty_: No separate announcement or advertising system. You can only say something if you're showing up. Presence validates the message.

**Key insight:** No separate vendor/commercial features needed. The unified session model handles commercial users naturally. Social pressure moderates abuse in a small community.

### Persona 5: Leah -- The Weekend Warrior Parent

_Gets one session per week. Needs to decide by Thursday evening whether Saturday is worth it to negotiate family schedule._

**[Role Playing #44]**: Tentative-to-Confirmed Flow
_Concept_: Leah posts a tentative intention for Saturday morning. On Friday evening, she confirms or cancels. The watching → planned → confirmed flow mirrors how real-world surf planning actually works over 1-2 days.
_Novelty_: Reflects the gradual commitment pattern rather than treating sessions as atomic events.

**[Role Playing #45]**: Cascade Confirmation
_Concept_: Leah posts tentative for Saturday. Others add their own tentative marks. Friday night, Leah confirms, others get a nudge (if opted in for that spot). Confirmations cascade through the group.
_Novelty_: Captures the social chain reaction of surf planning -- the same dynamic that currently happens in WhatsApp threads, but structured and visible.

**Key insight:** Leah validates the no-feed, efficiency-first philosophy. Time-poor users benefit most from signal-over-noise design.

### Persona 6: Tomas -- The Photographer

_Surfs and shoots photography. Posts best work on Instagram._

**[Role Playing #46]**: External Link in Profile
_Concept_: Tomas's ghost profile has an optional link to his Instagram. SpotApp points to external content platforms rather than competing with them.
_Novelty_: Acknowledges that content creation ecosystems already exist. The optional profile link serves photographers, instructors, shop owners -- anyone with an external presence.

**Key insight:** The app serves Tomas's surfing needs. His photography life lives elsewhere. SpotApp is a directory, not a gallery.

## Technique Execution: Cross-Pollination

Rapid-fire domain transfers from 11 unrelated products/platforms. Ideas were immediately categorized as MVP candidates or later-stage features.

**[Cross-Pollination #47]**: Quick-Vote Spot Conditions (from Waze)
_Concept_: A predefined list of transient conditions (seaweed, jellyfish, strong current, murky water, debris, crowded parking) that users can tap to vote on. The spot shows "3 reports of seaweed today." Votes auto-expire at end of day. No free text, just tap-to-report.
_Novelty_: Transplants Waze's one-tap hazard reporting but simplifies to a voting/tally model. Structured, aggregatable, zero-friction input.

**[Cross-Pollination #48]**: Activity-Backed Authorship (from Airbnb)
_Concept_: Wherever a username appears -- wiki edits, condition reports, session plans -- it's accompanied by their activity count at that specific spot. "Kalle (127 sessions)" next to a wiki edit. "Sofia (first visit)" next to a condition report. No scores, no badges -- just raw transparent history.
_Novelty_: Transplants Airbnb's trust-through-visibility but strips it to pure data. The reader's brain does the trust calculation.

**[Cross-Pollination #49]**: One-Tap Confirm (from Duolingo)
_Concept_: When arriving at a spot with a recent condition report, the primary action is a single button: "Conditions still accurate." Tap it and you've added your vote, refreshed the timestamp, increased the sample size. Only open sliders if conditions have changed.
_Novelty_: Transplants Duolingo's micro-completion insight. The default contribution path is one tap.

**[Cross-Pollination #50]**: Confirmation Count as Confidence (from Duolingo)
_Concept_: A condition report shows how many people confirmed it. "0.7m, W 8m/s -- reported by Kalle, confirmed by 3 others." Each confirmation refreshes recency. A report with 5 confirmations in the last hour is near-fact.
_Novelty_: The one-tap confirm creates a natural confidence score. No algorithm needed -- human consensus expressed through the simplest possible gesture.

**[Cross-Pollination #51]**: Historical Activity Patterns (from Google Maps) -- LATER STAGE
_Concept_: Over time, accumulated session data reveals spot rhythms as a "popular times" histogram. "Mellsten: busiest Sat 8-11am, quiet weekday afternoons." Emerges naturally from data.
_Novelty_: Transplants Google Maps' popular times built entirely from community session data.

**[Cross-Pollination #52]**: Condition-Triggered Notifications (from Slack) -- LATER STAGE
_Concept_: Because condition data is structured, users could set threshold alerts per spot: "Notify me when wind drops below 8 m/s at Spot X." Structured data architecture enables smart filtering.
_Novelty_: Transplants Slack's notification granularity into structured environmental data.

**[Cross-Pollination #53]**: The Craigslist Principle (from Craigslist)
_Concept_: Geography + recency = the entire information architecture. No algorithm, no curation, no "recommended for you." As the app grows, resist the pressure to add algorithmic sorting. The simplicity IS the competitive advantage.
_Novelty_: Transplants Craigslist's radical anti-algorithm stance into a modern map interface. A design commitment, not just a current choice.

**[Cross-Pollination #54]**: Auto-Generated Session Diary (from Strava)
_Concept_: Check-ins automatically build a personal session log: date, spot, sport, conditions reported that day. A private surf diary assembled from data that already exists. Zero additional effort.
_Novelty_: Transplants Strava's personal history as quiet motivation. Data is a byproduct of normal app use.

**[Cross-Pollination #55]**: Hyperlocal Condition Contrast (from Dark Sky)
_Concept_: Two spots 2km apart show completely different conditions because the community reports them independently. The map makes this visible at a glance. No interpolation, no averaging across spots. Each pin is its own micro-climate truth.
_Novelty_: Transplants Dark Sky's hyperlocal precision built from human reports instead of weather models. Granularity that forecast services can't achieve at coastline scale.

**[Cross-Pollination #56]**: Open Spot Data as Public Good (from OpenStreetMap) -- LATER STAGE
_Concept_: Over time, the community-built spot database becomes the most comprehensive water sports location dataset. Eventually expose as open API/exportable data.
_Novelty_: Transplants OpenStreetMap's volunteer-built-data-becomes-authoritative model.

**[Cross-Pollination #57]**: Two-Tier Contribution Model (from Shazam)
_Concept_: MVP has exactly two contribution paths. Tier 1: one-tap confirm ("still accurate"). Tier 2: full slider report (swell, wind direction, wind speed). No photos, no text, no in-between. The fastest possible useful contribution and the most detailed one.
_Novelty_: Transplants Shazam's "minimal input, maximum value" into the contribution model. Two paths, both structured, both aggregatable.

**[Cross-Pollination #58]**: Season Wrapped (from Spotify) -- LATER STAGE
_Concept_: Annual auto-generated summary from session data: total sessions, spots visited, sports breakdown, most-frequented spot. Shareable card for organic marketing.
_Novelty_: Transplants Spotify Wrapped. Zero effort to produce, high delight, viral growth mechanic.

**[Cross-Pollination #59]**: Wiki Edit Proposals (from GitHub) -- LATER STAGE
_Concept_: As communities grow, wiki edits from less-established users become proposals that experienced contributors can approve. Emerges as moderation layer when needed.
_Novelty_: Transplants open-source contribution governance into community knowledge management.

**[Cross-Pollination #60]**: The Session Tuple as Atomic Social Unit (from Venmo)
_Concept_: The entire social layer is built from one primitive: {user, spot, time, sport}. Session plans, check-ins, activity history, crowd indicators, cascade confirmations, ghost profiles -- all derived from this single object. No posts, no messages, no likes. Just sessions.
_Novelty_: Transplants Venmo's "transaction as social content" into its purest form. One data structure generates the entire social experience.

### MVP Core Candidates

Based on user decisions during the session, these ideas form the likely MVP feature set:

**Must-Have (Defines the Product):**
1. Map-first interface with zoom-density scaling (#16, #17)
2. Community-born spots with wiki pages (#1, #28)
3. Session planning: the session tuple {user, spot, time, sport} (#60, #3, #38)
4. Watching/tentative/confirmed session states (#33, #44)
5. Structured condition reporting with sliders (#14, #39)
6. One-tap confirm + confirmation count (#49, #50, #57)
7. Anonymous browse, register to contribute (#24, #29)
8. Ghost profiles with activity history (#18)
9. No social feed, no engagement mechanics (#25, #26, #27)

**Strong Candidates:**
10. At-a-glance spot cards on map (#32)
11. Recency indicators on condition data (#31)
12. Time-slider for forward planning (#6)
13. Sunset horizon default view (#20)
14. Activity-backed authorship (#48)
15. Quick-vote transient conditions (#47)
16. Auto-generated session diary (#54)
17. Opt-in per-spot notifications (#27)

### Ideas Banked for Later Stages

**Detail Design Phase:** Presets vs sliders UX, confidence decay on reports, disagreement signals, earned knowledge/reputation systems, safety check-in/check-out, per-spot notification subscription model, sport-aware condition display logic.

**Post-MVP Features:** Photo reports, social media bridging, vendor event pins, environmental API integration, historical activity patterns, condition-triggered notifications, season wrapped, wiki edit proposals, open data API.

### Product Philosophy (Confirmed Through All Techniques)

- **Map-first**: The map IS the app
- **Community-created**: Users build all content
- **Structured over unstructured**: Sliders and tuples, not chat
- **Signal over noise**: No feed, no likes, no streaks
- **Value before registration**: See everything, register to contribute
- **Multi-sport by default**: All water sports, one community
- **Ephemeral by design**: Data has tiered lifespans
- **Anti-algorithm**: Geography + recency = information architecture
- **Respect the user**: Anti-addictive, no engagement guilt

## Session Summary

**Session Achievements:**
- **60 ideas** generated across 3 techniques (SCAMPER, Role Playing, Cross-Pollination)
- **8 thematic clusters** identified with clear boundaries
- **17 MVP candidates** prioritized, **9 must-haves** identified
- **Strong product philosophy** crystallized and validated across all techniques
- **Clear MVP/later-stage boundary** established through user decisions during session

**Key Breakthrough:** The session tuple {user, spot, time, sport} as the atomic social unit (#60) -- a single data structure that generates the entire social experience without any traditional social media features. This is the architectural foundation of the product.

**Session Insight:** The user consistently made sharp scope cuts, saying "no" to features that would complicate the MVP. The resulting product vision is unusually focused for a brainstorming session -- a coordination tool with a clear anti-social-media philosophy built on structured data and community trust.
